Secret of perfect dough

First, we carefully choose the best components for our process: water, fresh yeast, premium flour, just a little of salt, and a tiny bit of olive oil. Inspired by our love of real tastes, we carefully form and knead the dough, letting it rest and rise to produce a pleasing fusion of textures. Our process relies heavily on temperature control to achieve perfect fermentation and a crust that precisely represents traditional pizza.

For the dough

Ingredients (per pizza):

  • 400 g strong white bread flour
  • 100 g fine ground semolina flour
  • 1/2 tbspsalt
  • 7 g sachet dried yeast
  • 1/2 tbsp caster (superfine) sugar
  • 350 ml lukewarm water

Preparation time: 45 minutes

Cooking time: 10-15 minutes

Technology:

  • To make the pizza, pour th flours and salt into a bowl and make a well in the center.
  • Add the yeast and sugar to the water, miz with a fork and leave for a few minutes.
  • Bring in all the flour, working your way towards the outher edges, mixing well.
  • When it start to come together, use your hands and pat it into a ball.
  • Knead the dough for around 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  • Flour the dough, cover with film and leave the rest for 30 minutes.
  • Roll the pizzas out about 30 minutes before you want to cook them.

Technology:

  1. When the dough is ready for baking, preheat the oven to 250°C (230°C fan) / 500F/ gas 9.
  2. Flour the surface, tear off a piece of dough and roll into a rough circle about 0.5 cm thick.
  3. Dust each one with a little flour and lay out on the surface.
  4. Drizzle each base with a little oil, add the tomato sauce (learn about sauce method in the next section) and season well.
  5. Place either directly on the bars of the oven or on a preheated baking sheet for 8-10 minutes until golden and crisp.
  6. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil, scatter over the rocket or other greens and serve hot.

Tomato Passata

Starting the fun process of creating the perfect tomato sauce for our favorite pizzas, we've found that the magic is in the simplicity of using premium ingredients. Our sauce starts with hand-picked tomatoes that have been sun-kissed and provide a burst of freshness. We infuse it with the heady dance of garlic, fragrant basil, and a dash of oregano to produce a flavorful symphony that perfectly embodies the goodness of handmade food. The alchemy is completed with a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil and a dash of sea salt, which results in a sauce that balances umami and sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 250 ml tomatoes
  • fresh basil
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tea spoon salt
  • black pepper
  • herbs

Technology:

  1. Carefully place the tomatoes in the boiling water for about 30 seconds to 1 minute.
  2. Transfer the blanched tomatoes to an ice-cold water bath. Once cooled, peel off skins.
  3. Cut the peeled tomatoes in half and scoop out the seeds with a spoon.
  4. Crush the tomatoes with your hands or use a blender to achieve the desired consistency.
  5. Pour the crushed tomatoes into a large saucepan. Simmer over low-medium heat to reduce and concentrate the flavors. Add salt and other seasonings to taste.
  6. Once the passata has reached the desired consistency, let it cool.
  7. Seal the jars tightly and store them in a cool, dark place.
  8. Use it fresh or store it for future use!

Other Tricks

  1. Mix dried oregano, garlic powder, or grated Parmesan cheese into the pizza dough before rolling it out.
  2. Combine mozzarella with a sharp cheddar, fontina, or even a hint of blue cheese.
  3. Slice the top off a garlic bulb, drizzle with olive oil, wrap in foil, and roast in the oven until soft. Squeeze the roasted cloves onto the pizza before baking for a sweet, mellow garlic essence.
  4. Crush the tomatoes with your hands or use a blender to achieve the desired consistency.
  5. Pour the crushed tomatoes into a large saucepan. Simmer over low-medium heat to reduce and concentrate the flavors. Add salt and other seasonings to taste.
  6. Reduced balsamic vinegar adds a touch of sweetness and acidity, enhancing the flavors of the toppings.
